About The Legend

Full Name: Mahendra Singh Pansingh Dhoni

Birth: July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Bihar (now Jharkhand), India

Family: He was born to Pan Singh and Devki Devi and has a sister, Jayanti Gupta, and a brother, Narendra Singh Dhoni. His family hails from Uttarakhand.

Education: Dhoni attended DAV Jawahar Vidya Mandir in Ranchi, where he excelled in various sports, including cricket, football, and badminton. He initially played as a goalkeeper in football but later focused on cricket.

Dhoni's cricketing career took off when he was selected for the Central Coal Fields Limited (CCL) team in 1998.

His international debut came on December 23, 2004, against Bangladesh in an ODI match.

He took over as captain of the Indian T20 team in 2007 and led India to victory in the inaugural ICC T20 World Cup.

T20 World Cup: 2007

ODI World Cup: 2011

Champions Trophy: 2013

Most matches as captain: Led India in 331 international matches.

Awards: Received prestigious awards including Padma Bhushan (2018), Padma Shri (2009), and ICC ODI Player of the Year (2008, 2009).

Retirement: On August 15, 2020.

Playing Style: Dhoni is known for his calm demeanor and strategic acumen on the field. He holds several records as a wicketkeeper, including being the first Indian to score over 4,000 runs in Test matches.

Despite a rocky start to his career, he quickly established himself with impressive performances, notably scoring 148 runs against Pakistan in just his fifth ODI.
